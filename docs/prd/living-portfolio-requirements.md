# PRD: Living Portfolio Requirements

## Summary

`living-portfolio` is a living career map for Sethuram. It should help visitors understand who he is, what kind of engineer he is, what he has built, what he is currently exploring, and how to contact him, while making the site memorable.

The portfolio should feel exploratory and distinct without becoming a game, hiding important information, or overwhelming recruiters.

## Audience Hierarchy

1. Craft evaluators: senior engineers, founders, hiring managers, collaborators, and others who can judge engineering taste, product thinking, learning trajectory, and shipped work.
2. Peers and community: people interested in following the journey, writing, learning, and experiments.
3. Recruiters: hiring-oriented visitors who need fast clarity and practical links.

Recruiters must be able to understand the portfolio without decoding the map metaphor.

## Core Positioning

The site should primarily communicate:

1. Sethuram is a thoughtful builder.
2. Sethuram has a distinct personality and memorable presentation.
3. Sethuram has active momentum through current building, writing, learning, and experiments.

The role signal should be backend-first, with light AI and frontend breadth. Older frontend-heavy projects can support craft and problem-solving proof, but the site should not position Sethuram as frontend-first.

## Primary Journey

The preferred visitor journey is:

1. Explore Sethuram's work.
2. Notice his current journey and active momentum.
3. Contact him or view resume details when ready.

Contact and resume access should remain available, but the site should not behave like a single CTA funnel.

## Required Surfaces

- Home / World Map: natural entry point, living portfolio premise, exploration, and direct access.
- Work / Inventory: substantial projects as inspectable Items.
- Experience / Milestones: professional career phases and backend engineering proof.
- Skills / Skill Tree: capabilities grouped meaningfully.
- Quest Log: current building, writing, learning, experiments, and completed milestones.
- Writing / Field Notes: notes, essays, technical writing, reflections, and dev logs.
- Contact / Social: resume, email/contact, GitHub, LinkedIn, and other relevant profiles.

## Exploration And Direct Access

The site must support both:

- Explore path: richer thematic navigation through the World Map, Inventory, Skill Tree, Quest Log, and related surfaces.
- Direct path: straightforward access to work, experience, resume, contact, role fit, and social profiles.

No visitor should be forced to use playful navigation to find important career information.

## Content Boundaries

Work should be separated into:

- Items: substantial projects with enough proof to inspect.
- Professional Milestones: completed work-history achievements.
- Field Notes: writing, learning, reflection, or dev-log material.
- Current Quests: in-progress building, writing, learning, or experiments.

Inventory Items require:

- A problem or motivation.
- A visible artifact.
- A short story explaining what Sethuram learned or why it mattered.

Current Quests, Field Notes, and learning Skill Nodes may be unfinished. Inventory Items and Professional Milestones should represent real, inspectable, completed proof.

## Map Metaphor Requirements

The World Map should be a navigation metaphor, not a simulation.

- Zones are clickable career/content areas.
- The map may feel spatial, playful, and exploratory.
- Important content must remain reachable through clear navigation and direct links.
- No required gameplay, puzzles, XP grind, or hidden-only content.

Professional experience should be integrated into the World Map as Career phase Zones and Professional Milestones, not isolated from the rest of the portfolio.

## Freshness Requirements

The site should feel alive through current work and dated activity, but it must not promise a fixed update cadence.

- Status HUD may show currently building, writing, and learning.
- Quest Log may show dated Current Quests, Field Notes, and Milestones.
- Stale dates are acceptable if they are honest.
- The site should not imply daily, weekly, or scheduled updates.

## Tone And Personality

The site should make visitors curious first, then confident.

- Personality should come through in naming, interaction, tone, and structure.
- Playful labels should be paired with clear plain-language meaning.
- Copy should feel warm and distinct, not corporate.
- Professional proof should remain credible and easy to scan.
- The site should avoid theme-only labels and jokes that reduce clarity.

## Non-goals

- Do not build a full game.
- Do not require gameplay to access content.
- Do not hide important career information behind interactions.
- Do not over-index on old college frontend projects.
- Do not commit to a fixed update cadence.
- Do not become a dense LinkedIn or resume clone.
- Do not present unfinished experiments as completed proof.
- Do not overwhelm recruiters with metaphor before role fit is clear.

## Success Criteria

A successful visit leaves someone understanding who Sethuram is, what kind of engineer he is, what he has built, what he is currently exploring, and how to contact him, while remembering the site afterward.
